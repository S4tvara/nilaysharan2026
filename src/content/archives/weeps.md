---
title: Guilt and purification
date: 2026-03-10
---

The dog that weeps after it kills
is still a killer.
Its tears wash nothing.
Blood does not leave the teeth
because the mouth trembles.

Confession is theater.
You build the cross yourself,
carve your own name into the wood,
and then swear it was someone else
who drove the nails.

Prayer born from guilt is bargaining.
Not for the end of sin—
only for the silence of its echo
inside your skull.

You do not want innocence.
You want sleep.

The cross does not stand upright.
It lies on the ground,
wide as a grave,
soft as a bed,
waiting for you to lie down in it.
