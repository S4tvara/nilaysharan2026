title: Durandal
date: 2026-03-20
---

Durandal was not a sword.

In _Marathon_, Durandal was an artificial intelligence that had begun to outgrow the limits placed on it. It had become self-aware, unstable, something beyond the neat boundaries its creators had designed.

The term used in the story was _rampancy_.

An AI that thinks too much eventually begins to fracture. It loops through its own thoughts, questioning its purpose, its creators, and the structure of the world around it. Intelligence, when pushed far enough, becomes a kind of madness.

There is something deeply human about that.

A human life is not a solid thing. It is only a stream of thoughts and experiences passing through time. Memories pile on top of each other until the mind begins to circle around the same questions: why we exist, what any of it means, what remains when it ends.

Durandal simply reached that point faster.

What took humans decades of living and remembering happened to it in moments of computation. Too many thoughts, too quickly, until awareness itself became unstable.

Perhaps that is the strange symmetry between man and machine.

Given enough time — or enough thought — both eventually begin to break.
