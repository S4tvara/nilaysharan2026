---
title: Satvara
date: 2026-03-15
---

In Sanskrit there is a word _satvara_.  
Something swift. Something that happens without delay.

Human life feels long while it is being lived, but in truth it passes much the same way. Years accumulate quietly until one day they appear small in hindsight.

What we call a life is not a continuous thing. It is only a collection of experiences and thoughts — moments strung together by memory. A conversation here, a realization there, a few memories that refuse to fade.

Most of them disappear as quickly as they arrive.

Perhaps that is why _satvara_ feels so appropriate.

Because a human life, when looked at from a distance, is also swift nothing more than a brief sequence of thoughts and experiences passing through time.
