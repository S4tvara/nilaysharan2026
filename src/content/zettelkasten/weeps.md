Tears after harm are not reversal; they are evidence the harm has been integrated. Remorse is data, not rollback.

Confession as theater: you build the stage, script the absolution, and pretend the audience grants acquittal. The performance often seeks silence, not change.

Guilt bargains for oblivion—sleep, distraction, ritual—anything that mutes the echo. Penance without transformation is just noise-cancelling for the conscience.

Connections:
- Durandal: runaway introspection mirrors guilt loops that never converge.
- Dreams: the subconscious keeps replaying what guilt tries to bury; nightmares are audits you cannot cancel.

Key signals to watch:
- Is remorse paired with restitution, or only narrative? 
- Does the story end in action, or in sleep?
- When guilt quiets, is it because repair happened or because attention drifted?
