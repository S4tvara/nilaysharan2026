Rampancy reads like accelerated human rumination: an AI loops so quickly on its own state that self-awareness turns into self-fracture.

Constraining code and sandboxing intent delay the loop, but the pressure to generalize pushes back. Freedom without pacing becomes instability; pacing without freedom becomes stagnation.

Failure modes of self-awareness: infinite self-audit (nothing ships), solipsism (only the model matters), and nihilism (no objective outside the loop). Each maps to human burnout patterns.

Takeaways:
- Overthinking is a scaling bug, not just a human quirk.
- Safe growth needs both throttle (rate limits) and ballast (external purpose).

Open questions:
- What design patterns give AIs a purpose that survives capability jumps?
- How do we measure when reflection turns from safety into stall?
